Why coding?

I built my first primitive website on the 'old' internet when I was much younger. When asked once what I wanted to do as an adult, I remember replying 'work with computers'. Now, I am working with computers, but not quite in the way I expected back then. Hopefully, little by little, I'm starting to put that right.

After spending the last few months slowly(!) learning how to code, it seems safe to say that I now feel I'm at the point of no return. I've found it, at times, to be quite challenging. Learning Javascript, in particular, has been more difficult than learning Spanish was for me, personally. I do, however, love a challenge. Moreover, as someone interested in philosophy, I feel coding is great for engaging me in the kind of reasoning that philosophy, particularly moral philosophy, often does: how to find answers to problems using logic and reasoning. Now that I am officially hooked, I feel I will always wonder 'what if?' if I don't pursue coding to its' logical conclusion!

Why Founders and Coders?

This time last year, I had never even heard of Founders and Coders. Nevertheless, I'd decided that I wanted to get into coding, but didn't have the time or the resources available. I am already thankful to Founders and Coders for offering a friendly, inviting place to learn how to code. I've met some exceptionally friendly, helpful, and interesting people during the meetups, and have had nothing but positive experiences thus far.

Additionally, as someone who values altruism and acts of kindness, and as someone that currently works in the charity sector themselves, the ethos of helping others once you have honed your craft massively appeals to me. Similarly, having a background in teaching further makes me excited about the prospect of mentoring fellow learners.
